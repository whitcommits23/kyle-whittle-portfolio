My Story

I studied Industrial/Organizational Psychology at Brigham Young University-Idaho, which gave me a foundation in how people learn, perform, and change, with a deep appreciation for the data behind it. After graduating in 2014, I joined Qualtrics as a customer support specialist and quickly discovered that the work I loved most wasn't resolving tickets. It was figuring out why customers struggled, then building the systems that made the problem disappear.

That realization sent me back to school. I completed a Master of Education in Instructional Design at the University of Utah in 2019, while simultaneously building out customer education programs at Qualtrics that would eventually generate $1.6MM in revenue and digitize over 70 courses on Skilljar. Those years taught me how to design for scale: not just build training, but build the operational infrastructure around it.

A stint at McMaster-Carr in Cleveland, Ohio sharpened my operations mindset. Managing workflows for 10,000+ daily orders with a team of 25 made me a systems thinker in the truest sense. I learned to obsess over throughput, process standardization, and the compounding return of well-designed SOPs.

I returned to SaaS and joined Canopy, where I built the company's customer education function from scratch: a knowledge base, LMS, video library, and two certification programs. I also led technical support and eventually directed all customer implementation and education globally. Along the way I completed an MBA at Quantic School of Business and Technology with a 4.0 GPA.

Today I lead Customer Knowledge and Content Operations at Entrata, overseeing a 1,500+ article knowledge ecosystem across 60+ product lines and driving AI-powered content strategy across the entire CX org. I use Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini daily, treating them as force multipliers for a lean team doing the work of a much larger one.

Principles

What I Believe

Work must be results-driven

It's not enough to create something for the sake of creating. Every program, article, or system I build is tied to a measurable outcome: adoption, retention, efficiency, revenue. If I can't define what success looks like, I go back to the problem statement.

The learner is always the customer

Whether I'm building for internal teams or external customers, the person who has to learn the thing is the one I design for. That means understanding their prior knowledge, their context, their constraints, and building something they'll actually use rather than something that just checks a box.

Professionalism builds trust

I've worked with adults across market research, healthcare, automotive, financial, and manufacturing industries. That breadth taught me to be flexible, direct, and to respect people's time. The work I deliver reflects that: clear, purposeful, and built to last.
